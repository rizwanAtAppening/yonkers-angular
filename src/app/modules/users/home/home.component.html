Selling or renting Your Property?

You will first need to obtain a certifiacte of occupancy

Before you sell any residential property in Morristown, you must obtain a Certificate of Habitability from the Division of Housing and Property Maintenance and a satisfactory Zoning Review from the Zoning Officer, for each dwelling unit. This applies to single-family homes, two-family homes, co-ops, apartments, condos, town homes, multi-family homes and dwelling units in mixed-use buildings.

If you have questions about the approval process or how to calculate your fees, please call Housing & Property Maintenance (973) 292-6700 on weekdays

How it Work

01

Complete and submit your Application and provide the necessary Supporting documentation including indentification, listing agreement, etc.

02

Pay the required fees

04

An inspection for/ housing property maintenance will be scheduled. A seprate inspection for smoke Detector, Carbon Monoxide Alarm and Fire Extinguisher. They could occur on different days. You and Your desing must be present for both inspections.

03

The Zoning taxes and assessors offices may perform a record review.

05

Once your permanent or temporary certificate is ready you and your desingee may pick it up or it may be mailed to you (depending on the Municipality)

06

Inspection will be recorded provided by the inspector and issued and your certificate will be issued. If there are violations you will have an opportunity to correct them request a temporary certificate.
